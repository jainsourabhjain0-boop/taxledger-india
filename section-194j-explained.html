Section 194J Explained: TDS on Professional Fees, Technical Services and More

20 June 2026 • Editorial Desk • 8 min read

A practical guide to section 194J covering applicability, threshold, rates, examples, due dates and common mistakes.

What is section 194J?

Section 194J of the Income-tax Act requires tax deduction at source on specified payments such as fees for professional services, fees for technical services, royalty, non-compete fees and director’s remuneration in certain cases. In practice, this section becomes relevant when a business makes payments to professionals like chartered accountants, lawyers, consultants, architects, designers, doctors, software specialists and technical service providers.

When does section 194J apply?

The obligation generally arises when a resident is paid professional or technical fees and the aggregate payment exceeds the prescribed threshold during the financial year. Businesses should track vendor-wise payments across the year rather than invoice by invoice, because the trigger is based on aggregate amount.

Rate of TDS and practical points

The applicable rate depends on the nature of payment. Businesses should collect PAN, verify whether the service falls under professional or technical category, and review whether any lower deduction certificate has been furnished. If PAN is not available, higher deduction provisions can apply. Always cross-check the latest rate notifications before deduction.

Examples

If a company pays its tax consultant professional fees during the year beyond the threshold, TDS under section 194J becomes relevant. If a business engages a technical consultant for specialized support or system design, the same section may apply depending on the facts. Director remuneration not chargeable under salary can also come within this section.

Compliance checklist

Deduct tax at the time of credit or payment, whichever is earlier. Deposit TDS within due date, report it in the correct quarterly TDS return, and issue TDS certificate in time. Reconcile vendor ledger, expense ledger and Form 26Q before filing to avoid mismatches.
